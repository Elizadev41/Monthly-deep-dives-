About This Project

Hi, I'm Iyin, a 14-year-old student with a curiosity for science, sports, spirituality, and the world around me.

Monthly Deep Dives started with a simple question about why mosquitoes are considered the deadliest animals in the world. As I researched, I realized how much there is to learn from the topics we often overlook.

After praying about the idea, I felt God leading me to create this space.

Through writing and research I explore questions about faith, life, science, sports, and everything in between. One topic. One month. One deep dive at a time.

Why This Matters

Curiosity is the engine behind every deep dive. If we only pay attention to the loudest topics, we miss the quiet ones that shape our lives, our health, and our decisions.

At Monthly Deep Dives, I choose questions that feel small at first but often have bigger meaning. A mosquito is not just an insect. It is a window into health, science, and how the world works. A sports story is not just about wins and losses. It is about practice, discipline, and what motivates people.

This project matters because it helps me and my readers practice a different kind of thinking: one that asks why, looks for evidence, and connects what feels ordinary to what is important.

That is why I care about topics that seem basic. When we understand the small stuff, we understand the bigger picture better.

What I Hope This Does

I want this journal to be a place where ideas grow instead of stopping at the first answer. I want readers to feel like they can ask hard questions, think clearly, and notice the things other people might not be talking about.

Whether it is science, faith, sports, or the world around us, this is a place to slow down, learn, and share what we discover.
